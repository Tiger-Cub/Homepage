Representation theory of finite groups and algebras

The course offers an introduction to the representation theory of finite groups and algebras.

News

The course will begin during the winter semester 2022-2023. The language of the course will be English.

Lectures

The schedule is to be determined. It will consist of approximately 14 lectures. The following is not a set schedule, and is meant to give a rough outlook of the contents of the course.

  • Lecture 1. Linear representations. Examples: one-dimensional representations, the regular representation, permutation representations. The category of representations: subrepresentations, sums, products and homomorphisms. Irreducibility and indecomposablility. Schur's Lemma.
  • Lecture 2. Characters. Properties of characters. Class functions. Orhogonality relations. Decomposition of the regular representation. Irreducible representations and conjugacy classes. Examples of character tables.

Queued: Tensor products of representations. Examples of character tables. Maschke's theorem. Artin--Wedderburn decomposition theorem for semisimple associative algebras. Faithful representations. Tensor powers of a faithful representation contain all irreducibles. Induction and restriction. Frobenius reciprocity. Induced characters and the Frobenius formula. More later. Partitions and their statistics. Young diagrams. Young symmetrisers. Specht modules through idempotents and full classification of irreducibles. Algebraic integers. Frobenius divisibility. Solvable groups. Burnside's Theorem on solvability of groups of order $p^aq^b$.
