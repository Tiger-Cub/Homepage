Representation theory of finite groups and algebras

The course offers an introduction to the representation theory of finite groups and algebras.

News

The course will begin during the winter semester 2022-2023.

Lectures

The schedule is to be determined. It will consist of approximately 14 lectures. The following is not a set schedule, and is meant to give a rough outlook of the contents of the course.

  • Lecture. Motivation and background for the course. Finite groups of small orders. Examples of representations. One-dimensional representations.
  • Lecture. The category of representations. Irreducible and indecomposable representations. Schur's Lemma and the inner-hom product.
  • Lecture. Characters. Properties of characters. Class functions. Orhogonality relations. Decomposition of the regular representation.
  • Lecture. Irreducible representations and conjugacy classes. Tensor products of representations. Examples of character tables.
  • Lecture. Maschke's theroem. Artin--Wedderburn decomposition for semisimple algebras.
  • Lecture. Faithful representations. Tensor powers of a faithful representation.
  • Lecture. Induction and restriction. Frobenius reciprocity. Induced characters and the Frobenius formula.
  • Lecture. Representations of symmetric groups and Young diagrams I.
  • Lecture. Representations of symmetric groups and Young diagrams II.
  • Lecture. Algebraic integers. Frobenius divisibility. Solvable groups. Burnside's Theorem.
