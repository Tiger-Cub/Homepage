Representation theory of finite groups and algebras

The course offers an introduction to the representation theory of finite groups and algebras.

News

The course will begin during the winter semester 2022-2023. The language of the course will be English.

Lectures

The schedule is to be determined. It will consist of approximately 14 lectures. The following is not a set schedule, and is meant to give a rough outlook of the contents of the course.

  • Lecture 1. Motivation and background for the course. Finite groups of small orders. Examples of representations. One-dimensional representations.
  • Lecture 2. The category of representations. Irreducibility and indecomposablility. Schur's Lemma and the inner-hom product.
  • Lecture 3. Characters. Properties of characters. Class functions. Orhogonality relations. Decomposition of the regular representation.
  • Lecture 4. Irreducible representations and conjugacy classes. Tensor products of representations. Examples of character tables.
  • Lecture 5. Maschke's theorem. Artin--Wedderburn decomposition theorem for semisimple associative algebras.
  • Lecture 6. Faithful representations. Tensor powers of a faithful representation contain all irreducibles.
  • Lecture 7. Induction and restriction. Frobenius reciprocity. Induced characters and the Frobenius formula.
  • Lecture 8. Representations of symmetric groups I. Partitions and their statistics. Young diagrams. Young symmetrisers.
  • Lecture 9. Representations of symmetric groups II. Specht modules through idempotents and full classification of irreducibles.
  • Lecture 10. Algebraic integers. Frobenius divisibility. Solvable groups. Burnside's Theorem on solvability of groups of order $p^aq^b$.
  • Lecture 11. Finite quivers and their associated algebras. Dynkin diagrams and representation type. Gabriel's theorem.
